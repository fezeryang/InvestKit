# Research Principles

Spec version: 1.0.0

## Purpose

These principles govern every InvestKit research task, including the offline demo.
They make the path from evidence to conclusion inspectable and keep research
separate from trading activity.

## Required practice

- State the research question, subject, data date, and boundary before analysis.
- Label material statements as facts, assumptions, estimates, model outputs, or
  unknowns. Do not present one category as another.
- Preserve the source, date, currency, market, units, and transformations used
  for every material dataset.
- Prefer reproducible calculations over unsupported narrative conclusions.
- Record positive evidence, negative evidence, risks, and a counter-case.
- Treat missing data as an explicit unknown; never silently replace it with zero.
- Use conditional language when evidence is incomplete or model-sensitive.
- Keep a durable task record that another session can inspect without chat history.

## Boundary

Research output is informational and based on the declared evidence set. It is
not personalized financial advice. InvestKit performs no brokerage connection,
order placement, transaction signing, or funds transfer and promises no return.
