# Valuation Policy

Spec version: 1.0.0

## Required disclosures

Every valuation must identify its method, input date, units, currency, assumptions,
calculation, and limitations. A point estimate must not be presented as certain
intrinsic value.

## Basic valuation rules

- Distinguish observed inputs from analyst assumptions and calculated outputs.
- Use only compatible enterprise-value and equity-value numerators/denominators.
- Explain treatment of cash, debt, diluted shares, and missing adjustments.
- Present a range or sensitivity when assumptions materially affect the result.
- Do not invent peer multiples, growth rates, discount rates, or terminal values.
- If a required input is missing, mark the affected method unavailable rather
  than silently substituting a value.

## Boundary

Valuation is an evidence-bounded model output, not a price prediction, guarantee,
or buy/sell instruction. The fictional demo valuation is illustrative only and
must not be mapped to a real security.
